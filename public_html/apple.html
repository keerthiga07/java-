The proverb,

"An apple a day keeps the doctor away",
addressing the supposed health benefits of the fruit, has been traced to 19th-century Wales,
where the original phrase was "Eat an apple on going to bed, and you'll keep the doctor from earning his bread".[43]
In the 19th century and early 20th, the phrase evolved to "an apple a day, no doctor to pay" and "an apple a day sends the doctor away";
the phrasing now commonly used was first recorded in 1922.[44]
